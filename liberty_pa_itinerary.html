PA Grand Canyon — Leonard Harrison State Park
Around 9:30 AM
~45 min from Liberty via Rt 287 N → Rt 660 W
One of the most dramatic views in Pennsylvania — a 1,450-foot gorge carved by Pine Creek. The overlook at Leonard Harrison is just a few minutes' walk from the parking lot (paved, easy), with a visitor center, concessions, and restrooms on site. Worth the drive for the view alone.

For those who want more trail: The Turkey Path descends 1.7 miles and 800 feet to the canyon floor at Pine Creek — steep, rewarding, and about 2–3 hours round trip. At the bottom you can connect to the Pine Creek Rail Trail (flat gravel) and wander as far as you like before climbing back up.

One easy approach: hikers drop down the Turkey Path while anyone who prefers can enjoy the overlook, explore the rim trail (1.5 mi, very gentle), grab a snack from the concessions stand, and soak up the views until everyone reconvenes at the parking lot.

Optional: Cherry Springs Stargazing
After dinner (9:30 PM+)
Optional
Cherry Springs State Park is one of the darkest skies east of the Mississippi — the Milky Way is visible with the naked eye on a clear night. It's a late commitment (drive out after dinner, arrive around 11 PM), but unlike most stargazing spots, it requires no hiking — just blankets, chairs, and lying on your back looking up.

What to bring: warm layers (summer nights get cold up there), blankets or reclining chairs, red flashlights only (white light ruins night vision for everyone). Check the forecast and moon phase beforehand — a night close to the new moon is ideal.
~1 hr 30 min each way via Rt 44 S toward Coudersport. Best Friday or Saturday; pick the clearer night.

McIntyre Wild Area — Rock Run
Around 10 AM
~35 min from Liberty via Rt 14 S toward Roaring Branch
The local gem. Rock Run is a stunning crystal-clear creek running through a forested hollow, with waterfalls, swimming holes, and dramatic terrain. Rated 4.8★. Trails run anywhere from 3 to 6+ miles depending on how far you follow the creek. Expect creek crossings and some overgrown sections — trail shoes and bug spray are essential.

Those who'd rather have a relaxed morning can skip this one and explore Liberty, walk the path along the trout stream at Liberty Community Park, or hang around the house with a book. The McIntyre crew can plan to be back by early afternoon.
Reviews mention black rattlesnakes in the area. Stay on trail and watch your footing around rocky outcrops — they're not aggressive but worth knowing about.

If It Rains

Pennsylvania in late June can bring afternoon storms. Here's how to pivot without losing the day.

📚 Home Base Day

The house was booked for a reason. Lean into it: books, board games, movies, padel in the drizzle (actually fun). Sometimes a rain day becomes the most remembered one.

🏛 Wellsboro Main Street

A well-preserved Victorian downtown with antique shops, boutiques, a diner, and covered awnings. Easy to browse in light rain, about 45 min away.

🍺 Yorkholo Brewing

A beautiful restored building in Mansfield (~30 min). Trivia nights, great food, local craft beers. A perfect afternoon refuge if the weather turns.

🪨 Liberty Indian Museum

A small local museum right in Liberty covering Native American history, beadwork, and artifacts. Rated 4.9★ — an underrated rainy-hour stop steps from the house.

🌊 Hike Anyway

Waterfalls are actually better in the rain. Jacoby Falls or McIntyre in light rain can be genuinely magical — bring rain jackets and embrace it.

🎬 Movie Block

Make popcorn, pick something terrible together, and let D & S have first pick. Underrated family bonding, every time.

Good to Know

  • Martin's Pantry (Rt 414, Liberty) — part deli, part general store, part soft-serve ice cream counter. Your best local grocery stop. Stock up Thursday on arrival. Closed Sundays.
  • Bug spray is non-negotiable at McIntyre Wild Area. Reviews specifically call this out. Long sleeves help in the hollow.
  • Cell service can be spotty in the hollows and on trails. Download offline maps (AllTrails or Google Maps offline) before heading out.
  • Trail shoes or waterproof hikers are needed for McIntyre and Jacoby Falls — both have creek crossings. Sneakers will get soaked.
  • Leonard Harrison visitor center has concessions, a gift shop, and restrooms on site — reopened in 2024 after renovation. No need to bring your own coffee to the canyon.
  • Cherry Springs tip: Check the moon phase before committing. A full or near-full moon washes out the Milky Way. Within a few days of the new moon is ideal.
  • The Roost (Wellsboro) is a great lunch spot but doesn't open until noon on Fridays and Saturdays. Closed Mondays.
  • Rattlesnakes are present at McIntyre and Golden Eagle Trail. Stay on marked trails, don't reach under rocks. Not aggressive — just worth knowing.
  • Padel basics: Serves must bounce once before hitting the back wall. Points play like tennis. The learning curve is gentle — you'll be rallying within 20 minutes.
  • Approximate drives from Liberty: Wellsboro ~45 min · Leonard Harrison ~45 min · McIntyre ~35 min · Cherry Springs ~1.5 hr · Jacoby Falls ~25 min · Farmer's Table ~20 min · Yorkholo ~30 min.
